Signals and Systems

ECE2111 teaches the mathematical foundations for signal processing, control systems, communication systems as well as basic Fourier tools and Laplace transforms.

This unit can potentially guide you to a wide variety of engineering sub-disciplines such as:

Computer Vision Medical Imaging Technology Control System Design

Why is Signal Processing Important?

  • Signals in the real world are susceptible to noise through various means. For instance, physical objects such as buildings and electrical wires can all contribute (or be the source of) noise to a signal.

  • Noise is undesirable as it can lead to several errors in the received signal. To eliminate noise, Fourier Transform can be used to find which frequencies are dominant in a noisy signal, i.e. separating the noise from the essence of the signal.

  • Once the dominant frequencies are found, an inverse Fourier Transform can be applied to obtain the original signal without the noise.

Prerequisite knowledge?

  • ENG 1060 : students are expected to know the basic fundamentals of calculus and MATLAB programming

  • ENG 1091 or ENG 1005 : students are expected to know manipulation of complex numbers, function notation and basic algebra
